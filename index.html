About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Oklahoma State University, advised by Dr. H. B. Acharya, with an expected graduation in 2028. My research centers on in-network computation and data-plane programming in P4, programmable data planes for network security, and network, Internet, and censorship measurement. I build high-performance packet processors on the Intel Tofino (TNA) platform that approach the capability of application-layer firewalls and network intrusion detection systems, and I design tools to detect and measure Internet censorship.

Beyond research, I am active in academic service and teaching: I serve as President of the Computer Science Graduate Student Association at OSU, and I have taught as an adjunct instructor and teaching assistant across several security and systems courses.

Research

In-Network Computation Using Data-Plane Programming in P4 2021–Present

Developing high-performance packet processors comparable to application-layer firewalls and network intrusion detection systems; implementing advanced algorithms for packet analysis and filtering in P4 on the Intel Tofino (TNA) platform.

Internet Censorship Measurement 2021–Present

Designed and implemented tools for detecting and measuring Internet censorship across multiple countries; published measurement findings on the Russia–Ukraine conflict’s impact on Internet resilience (ASIACCS 2023).
